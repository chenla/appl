** Introduction

/Tolerance/ and her sister apsara /compromise/ is an
obligation that makes cooperation possible and is one of the
cornerstones of civilization.

See links below until I can block out the problem more.
Watch Aurora's presentation on Youtube.

This is a good start, but it's very much something from this moment in
time and history -- need to think about this in a broader LOC and
longer POV.

The West is so tied up in knots at the moment about /identity/ that
#2 feels like it's not going to age well.

I also feel that the emphasis on targeting speech and actions against
members of or multiple members of a /group/ is not the right way
forward.  The US constition was careful to speak of the individual.
The execptions came later -- gender, or slavery or race treated as a
group.  Political parties are treated as groups, age is treated as
different groups.

Blanket rules that apply equally to all groups is not a bad idea, but
it hasn't really been tried institutionally except for the classes
mentioned above.  Aurora makes some excellent arguments.

I think Doctorow's final comment about automated application of rules
was good.  But there is no way to scale these things beyond human
cognitive scales.  These are things that shouldn't be scaled and the
solution is to keep groups within sizes where these things do work.

Google, Facebook and Twitter scales will never work.  Superpower
scales will never work, at least for long.  Their complexity makes
these systems unstable.

We could take an approach that applies such rules at different scales.
Different sized groups should be treated differently, majority is not
the same as minority.  Global and local scales are not the same.
Individual and groups are different.

** The Intolerable Speech Rule

  1. Advocating for the removal of human rights
  2. From people based on an aspect of their identity
  3. In the context of systemic oppression primarily harming that
